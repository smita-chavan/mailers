What You Need To Know About Digital Marketing in 2021
Digital marketing is a career ripe with potential. The pandemic has not only accelerated brands’ move to digital, but reinstated the message that digital marketing remains indispensable for the future growth of businesses across industries.

Moreover, according to a recent US Bureau Of Labour Statistics (BLS) report, employment of marketing
managers is set to grow 9% by 2024, which is faster than the average for all occupations. This growth is due to the increased use of social media and internet marketing. Which again makes digital marketing an extremely viable career.

Upskilling, Learning, Transitioning: Empowering a Career Switch
While Covid-19 may have brought the world to a standstill, the pandemic didn’t stop employees from switching jobs in 2020. The 2021 IBM CEO Study shows that 1 in 5 employees voluntarily changed employers in 2020. The report also shows that finding more purpose and meaning in daily work ranked as the second-highest reason for the switch. The same held for Andrew Woller, an ex–sales and account manager in 2020 and a full-time digital marketer now in 2021. This is the story of Andrew Woller, who reclaimed his passion for marketing and ventured into digital marketing from a sales-driven role.
